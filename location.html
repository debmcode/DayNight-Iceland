Iceland

Location

Three maps of Iceland are provided here. First a full view of Iceland, located in the northern region of the Atlantic Ocean. Next a view of Iceland’s capital Reykjavík, located in southwestern Iceland. Following is an illustrated map of Iceland; hover on city names for additional visitor information.

Saudarkrokur has a popular horse riding show every weekend, heated swimming pools, and great skiing.
Akureyri is home to an annual skiing and snowboarding festival, and the largest brewery in the country.
Egilsstadir is reknowned for it’s excellent hiking tours that lead to scenic locations which include waterfalls, lakes, canyons, and caves.
Borgarnes is a fascinating historical settlement over 1,000 years old, and an essential gateway to the Snaefellsnes Peninsula.
Grindavik is home to the famous Blue Lagoon Geothermal Spa where visitors can enjoy rest and relaxation in style.
Vatnajokull is Europe’s largest glacier. Breathtaking ice cave tours and a visit to sparkling Diamond Beach, on the Jokulsarlon Glacial Lagoon are a must.
